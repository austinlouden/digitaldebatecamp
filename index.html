about

Debate institutes are expensive. With the combined costs of room and board, tuition, and travel, attending a summer debate camp is an exclusive opportunity. That's why we created the Digital Debate Camp, bringing in some of the best debaters from around the country to offer all the resources of a traditional institute at the fraction of the cost.

We use technology to provide a fully immersive camp experience. Students enjoy all of the same educational benefits of a traditional camp: an extensive lecture series from a wide-range of nationally successful debaters about a vast array of topics, research sessions, and practice debates to prepare students for the coming season.

faq

Q: What are the dates for the camp?
A: Camp officially begins June 1st and ends August 16th.

Q: How much does the Digital Debate Camp cost?
A: $450. Please contact us about scholarships or financial assistance!

Q: What types of debate are taught at the Digital Debate Camp?
A: We primarily instruct cross-examination policy debate, though we have had several students use the camp for general debate knowledge, Lincoln-Douglas and Public Forum.

Q: What if I have to miss a few weeks of camp for vacation or other institutes?
A: We anticipate that a lot of our students sign up for our camp because they have many other things to take care of over the summer, and we try our best to accommodate that - we want students to be able to enjoy camp and gain from it whenever they have the time to do so. We keep all the lectures and files on record, so students can easily retrieve them when they are back from vacation. The camp runs on 2-week cycles so students can jump right back into the action when they are ready to return.

Q: Do you get all the camp files at the end of the summer?
A: Every member of the camp will receive copies of all files and lectures, which will also be stored in a central location for easy access both during the summer and throughout the year.

Q: How do practice debates work?
A: Practice debates happen in a few ways: debaters can self-organize and the camp will provide a judge for the round; instructors will have an availability calendar in which they will organize debates; the camp will hold several round robins and tournaments for debaters to attend. Using web cams and Google hangouts, we recreate the traditional debate experience online.
